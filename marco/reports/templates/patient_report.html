Glossary

Gene A section of your DNA that provides instructions for your body. Some genes control how your body breaks down (metabolizes) medications.
Diplotype The specific combination of two gene copies you inherited — one from each parent. Your diplotype determines how the gene functions.
Phenotype The observable effect of your gene combination on how your body processes medications.
Normal Metabolizer Your body breaks down the medication at a typical rate. Standard doses are usually appropriate.
Intermediate Metabolizer Your body breaks down the medication somewhat slower than typical. A dose adjustment may be needed.
Poor Metabolizer Your body has significant difficulty breaking down the medication. An alternative drug or a major dose change may be needed.
Ultrarapid Metabolizer Your body breaks down the medication much faster than typical. Standard doses may be insufficient, or the drug may not work well.
