Methodology

  • Analysis Tool: PharmCAT (Pharmacogenomics Clinical Annotation Tool)
  • Reference Genome: GRCh38/hg38
  • Guidelines: CPIC (Clinical Pharmacogenetics Implementation Consortium), DPWG (Dutch Pharmacogenetics Working Group)
  • Symbol Key: ▲ Action (clinical attention needed) • ◆ Review (monitor/discuss) • ✓ Normal (standard response) • — No Data (insufficient information)
  • Limitations: Analysis is limited to variants present in the input VCF and covered by PharmCAT's allele definitions. Structural variants, copy number variations, and novel alleles may not be detected. CYP2D6 structural variation is particularly challenging from short-read sequencing data. HLA typing requires specialized tools not included in this pipeline.

Disclaimer

This pharmacogenomic report is generated from automated computational analysis and is provided for clinical decision support purposes only. Results should be interpreted in the context of the patient's complete clinical picture, including medical history, current medications, renal/hepatic function, and other relevant factors.

Pharmacogenomic testing does not replace clinical judgment. Prescribing decisions should not be based solely on genetic test results. This report does not account for drug-drug interactions, environmental factors, or epigenetic modifications that may influence drug response.

For questions about specific drug-gene interactions or dosing recommendations, consult CPIC Guidelines or contact a clinical pharmacogenomics specialist.
